# Executive communication - three bullet points, last two don't matter

---
name: Executive communication principle
description: Three bullet points for execs — make sure the last two don't matter. First bullet IS the message.
type: feedback
originSessionId: d0200357-680c-44a8-8c59-57ac6fb7653e
---
"Give them 3 bullet points to look at, and make sure the last two don't matter."

**Why:** 80%+ of managers/execs are fast-scan thinkers. They read the first bullet, glance at the rest to confirm there's substance, and move on. The first bullet IS the message. The other two exist to make it look like a list instead of a lone claim.

**How to apply:**
- Boss Summary format should be the default output for any tool that generates reports for management (standup_tool, time_tool story, status updates)
- Lead with the number/outcome that matters most
- Long-form narrative is the appendix, not the output
- Never open with context or background — open with the result
- Jira links and screenshots are better than paragraphs — they can click if they want depth
