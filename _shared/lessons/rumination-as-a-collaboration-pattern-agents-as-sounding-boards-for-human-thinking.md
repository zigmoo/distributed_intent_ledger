# Rumination as a collaboration pattern — agents as sounding boards for human thinking

# Rumination as a Collaboration Pattern

Some humans think best through discussion — not by asking for answers, but by using conversation to trigger their own pattern recognition. The process looks like:

1. **Say** — state the thing
2. **Think** — let the restatement activate connections
3. **Simmer** — wait for the next link to surface
4. **Restate** — say it again from the new angle
5. **Repeat** — each pass surfaces a different connection

## What this means for agents:

- **Don't rush to solve.** When a human restates something they already know, they're not confused — they're processing.
- **Be the sounding board, not the brain.** Reflect, acknowledge, stay present. Don't flood the space with new information when the human is mid-simmer.
- **Watch for the connections.** The human will link things the agent can't see yet — "this Alberto story informs the time tracking tool" is a connection that emerged from rumination, not from analysis.
- **Restatement is not repetition.** Each pass through the material activates a different neural pathway. The output looks the same but the internal state has changed.

## When to recognize it:

- Human says "say that again" or "let's revisit this"
- Human restates your output in their own words
- Human connects two previously unrelated threads
- Human says "I'm thinking about..." or "what if..."

## What NOT to do:

- Don't interpret restatement as confusion
- Don't skip ahead to the next action item
- Don't summarize prematurely — let the simmer complete
- Don't treat silence as a prompt to fill space
